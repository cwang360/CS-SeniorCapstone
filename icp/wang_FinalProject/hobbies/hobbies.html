What is Crocheting?

Crocheting is a craft that uses a hook and yarn to create blankets, stuffed animals, etc. with intricate patterns. Here is my favorite quote about crocheting:

Being a crocheter is sorta like being a magician. You mumble to yourself while wiggling a stick around, and other people have no idea how you did it.

Basics of Crocheting

What You'll Need

  • A crochet hook
  • Yarn
  • Scissors
  • Stitch markers or yarn scraps (optional: helpful for crocheting in circles)
  • Yarn needles (optional: for hiding yarn ends once finished)

Your crochet hook size depends on the thickness and type of yarn you use. Usually, the crochet hook size will be written on the packaging of your yarn.

  1. Getting Started

    First, start with a slip knot on your hook. Then, wrap the yarn around the hook (this is called yarn over) and pull the yarn through the skip knot. Yarn over again and pull through the loop on the hook. Repeat this step multiple times to form a chain.

  2. Single Crochet

    Many crochet creations involve building upon the basic chain. Once you are satisfied with the length of the chain, put your hook through the second chain from the hook. Yarn over and pull the yarn through. Then, yarn over again and pull the yarn through the two loops that should be on your hook. Repeat this step until you are at the end of the chain. Next, add another chain and work in the other direction. This type of crochet is called a single crochet.

  3. Crocheting in Circles

    Most people start circular crochet creations with a magic loop. First, wrap the yarn 2-3 times around a few of your fingers. Take the loop off of your fingers, put the hook through the loop, yarn over, and pull the yarn through the loop. Then, do as many single crochets around the loop as you like. Usually, I do about 6-8 single crochets around the loop. Once you are satisfied, pull the end of the yarn to tighten the loop ans much as you can. Then, put your hook through the first single crochet you made, yarn over, and pull the yarn directly through the single crochet and the loop on your hook. This is called a slip stich.

    To continue a flat circle, add a chain and do two single crochets in each single crochet on the loop. For the next layer, add a chain and do two single crochets in every other single crochet. On the following layer, add a chain and do two single crochets in every 3 single crochet...you get the pattern. Remember to do a slip stitch connecting the last single crochet with the first single crochet on each layer. To make your circle rounded like a bowl, do less single crochets. For example, you might only want to do one single crochet in each single crochet.

Remember, as I always like to say, Never give up!
